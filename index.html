My art process

First I construct an armature of wood strips, reed, bamboo, or wire. Then I build up the sculpture’s surface by applying small strips of tissue paper adhered with diluted glue. Each layer of tissue paper shrinks as it dries, providing significant compression strength. Finished lamps have as many as twelve layers of paper, and are amazingly strong and durable despite their delicate appearance. Some are painted with acrylic colors. Each sculpture is amply ventilated and provided with secure attachments for mounting.

Natural materials

Bent wood, reed, or driftwood create structure.

Paper

Many layers of thin, translucent paper are applied.

Installation options

Sculptures range in size and are free-standing or wall-mounted.
